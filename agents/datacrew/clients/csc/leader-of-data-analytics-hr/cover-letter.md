# CSC — Leader of Data Analytics: People and Culture (HR)

**Position:** Leader of Data Analytics: People and Culture (Human Resources)

**Company:** CSC (Corporation Service Company)

**Location:** Wilmington, DE (Hybrid — Mon–Fri 8am–5pm)

**Source:** https://www.indeed.com/viewjob?jk=d6b0db35e3097166

Hello!

The "Leader of Data Analytics" title caught my eye because it describes exactly what I did at Sony PlayStation, just with a different label.

At Sony, I built and led a Domo Center of Excellence from the ground up for our PaaS business. What started as a small team "keeping the lights on" for two or three business cases evolved into a program that positioned data as an accelerator across the organization. We bridged IT, Infosec, and Risk with business teams who needed data to make decisions — which is really what this role is asking for: someone who can translate complex data into executive-ready insights that actually drive action.

A few things from the job description that map directly to what I've done:

**Cross-functional partnering** — As the hub in cross-functional teams, our COE model was built on partnering across business units and departments — HR, Finance, governance, engineering, etc. At SIE our COE team called ourselves "pretend-gineers" – we were generalist specialists that had to broker conversations between technical and non-technical stakeholders but also guide and inform delivery and standards. Being honest, early on, I frequently felt the pressure to be 'the smartest person in the room', but quickly realized I needed to be better about finding smarter people to drive initiatives. By the time I left SIE, our goal to build generalists with several areas of specialist knowledge meant each of the specialists knew other true engineering and business unit specialists and so we were able to accelerate delivery across many areas. This role appeals to me because it sounds like the organization values and views strong cross-functional relationships as accelerators, and that's an environment I'd like to see myself in.

**AI and advanced analytics** — I've been building AI automation workflows for the past year, including agentic AI systems for knowledge work and coding tasks. I'm comfortable articulating both the value and the limitations of these approaches to stakeholders who aren't data scientists. I love the generalist specialist model and suspect that an organization hiring at the intersection of analytics, people and culture recognizes that modern work is being reshaped by advancements in technology and AI. This role sounds like it recognizes an exciting opportunity to change expectations about how we conceptualize knowledge work to embrace new working and business models.

**People and Culture** — Full disclosure, I am what Vanessa Van Edwards would call a classic "recovering awkward person". I am an engineer and tinker-er at heart. I used to be (and to an extent still am) the nerdy coder working on weekend coding projects for fun, but I also embrace community and co-learning and collaboration. I love organizing hackathons, "build days", webinars and am the founder of a slack community. I am not for everyone, but I love facilitating and supporting individual and team growth. I would tell my teammates "there's so much variety in work you can do there's no reason work shouldn't be fun." We would use hackathons and "build days" to empower teams to automate the repetitive tasks so they could tackle 'the fun stuff' – and once again it seems like this role has that bent.

**Data governance** — At Sony, compliance and privacy weren't afterthoughts — at a company that size, risk management was actually more important than speed – much to business teams’ chagrin.  When I started at SIE, Domo initially felt like a shadow-it purchase, but we quickly learned that scaling fast and safely meant partnering with experts instead of avoiding them!  By the time I left SIE, our COE had built completed several projects to proactively provide Infosec, Governance and Risk appropriate monitoring workflows and tools to keep our users safe while maximizing the business teams’ freedom to play and explore new workflows with data.  Honestly, the best part was that by improving our partnering we were to handover responsibility for risk management decisions back to the experts. 

I believe my experience translates to this role. I'd welcome the conversation to see if we’re a good fit!

Thank you for your time, Jae Wilson
