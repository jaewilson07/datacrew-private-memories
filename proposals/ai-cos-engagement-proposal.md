# AI Chief of Staff — Consulting Engagement Proposal

**Prepared by:** Jae Wilson, DataCrew
**Date:** July 2026
**Audience:** Consulting Practice Manager

---

## Executive Summary

Traditional consulting delivers point-in-time analysis — binders, decks, and recommendations that go stale the moment they're presented. The AI Chief of Staff (AI CoS) is a different model: a continuous, AI-native consulting engagement that runs operational intelligence daily, not quarterly.

The engagement combines structured research, KPI/OKR foundation building in Domo, and an ongoing AI layer that monitors, alerts, and advises. The consultant designs and governs; the AI executes and scales.

**What we're asking for:** Approval to productize this engagement model, with a defined build phase to create the reusable tooling that makes each engagement fast to deploy and profitable to run.

---

## 1. The Problem

Mid-market companies ($10M-$500M revenue) face a structural gap in operational intelligence:

- **They can't afford a dedicated analytics team.** A full-time data scientist costs $120-180K. Most mid-market CFOs make do with spreadsheets and gut feel.
- **Traditional consulting is a one-shot delivery.** A Wipfli or McKinsey engagement produces a binder — a snapshot in time. The moment the consultant leaves, the analysis starts decaying. Nobody updates it. Six months later, it's a dusty PDF.
- **They have Domo (or similar BI) but don't use it well.** The tool is paid for, the data is connected, but nobody has the time or expertise to build meaningful KPI dashboards. The platform becomes an expensive status board instead of a decision engine.
- **They don't know what they don't know.** Without industry benchmarks, a CFO can't tell whether their 120-day cash-to-cash cycle is normal or a problem. They don't have a frame of reference.

The result: executives make decisions based on incomplete, stale, or uncontextualized data. Not because they're not smart — because the infrastructure for continuous, benchmarked operational intelligence doesn't exist at their scale.

---

## 2. The Solution

The AI CoS engagement delivers three things in sequence:

1. **Foundation** — The right KPIs, benchmarked against industry peers, deployed in Domo
2. **Continuous Intelligence** — An AI layer that runs daily analysis on top of those KPIs
3. **Ongoing Advisory** — A consultant who reviews AI output, provides strategic context, and adjusts the system

The key difference from traditional consulting: **the AI runs continuously.** It doesn't leave after the binder. It monitors KPIs, detects anomalies, surfaces trends, and generates daily/weekly briefs — all governed by a consultant who reviews, contextualizes, and advises.

### What Makes It Different

| Dimension | Traditional Consulting | AI CoS |
|-----------|------------------------|--------|
| Delivery | Point-in-time (binder, deck) | Continuous (daily brief, live dashboards) |
| Research | Manual, per-engagement, non-reusable | AI-driven, cached per industry, compounding |
| Cost to deliver | High (consultant hours) | Low (AI does the work, human reviews) |
| Freshness | Stale within 30 days | Real-time / daily |
| Benchmarking | Limited (consultant's personal experience) | Structured (industry research + benchmark databases) |
| Scale | 1 consultant per 1-2 engagements | 1 consultant manages 10-20 engagements |
| Client retention | Low (engagement ends) | High (ongoing retainer) |

## 3. The Engagement

### Phase 1: Discovery (Weeks 1-3)

**Goal:** Understand the client's business, validate the problem, and design the KPI foundation.

| Week | Activity | Who | Deliverable |
|------|----------|-----|-------------|
| 1 | **Engagement Prep** — Industry assessment, company assessment, pattern hunting, synthesis | AI (runbook) | Engagement prep artifacts (worry matrix, pre-interview hypotheses, tailored forcing questions, KPI candidates, interview guide) |
| 2 | **Discovery Interviews** — 60-90 min with CFO, 30 min with operations lead | Consultant (human) | Interview notes, validated premises, data source map |
| 3 | **KPI Feasibility Assessment** — Score candidate KPIs against data availability, format, quality, Domo connectivity | AI + Consultant | KPI feasibility matrix, prioritized implementation roadmap |

**What the client sees:** A consultant who walks in already knowing their industry, their company's specific challenges, and the right questions to ask. Not a generic "tell me about your business" — but "Ryan, you're managing $500M across two acquired divisions with different systems. Do you have a single view of cash-to-cash cycle across both, or are you pulling spreadsheets?"

That specificity is the product of the engagement prep runbook — a 5-step process that combines industry research, company research, pattern hunting, and synthesis into a tailored interview guide. The research is done by AI, reviewed by the consultant, and takes 2-4 hours instead of 2-4 days.

**Phase 1 deliverables:**
- Engagement prep document (industry context, company analysis, worry matrix, hypotheses)
- Discovery interview report (validated premises, data source map, pain point inventory)
- KPI feasibility matrix (scored KPIs with implementation roadmap)
- Recommended KPI set (5-10 KPIs, benchmarked, with data source mapping)

### Phase 2: Foundation (Weeks 4-6)

**Goal:** Build the KPI/OKR foundation in Domo and deploy the dashboard.

| Week | Activity | Who | Deliverable |
|------|----------|-----|-------------|
| 4 | **Data Pipeline Setup** — Connect Domo to client data sources (ERP, CRM, financials), validate data quality | Consultant + AI | Data pipeline documentation, connector configuration |
| 5 | **Dashboard Build** — Deploy industry-specific KPI dashboard templates, configure alerts, set benchmark targets | AI + Consultant | Live KPI dashboard in Domo |
| 6 | **Review & Refine** — Walk client through dashboard, adjust KPIs, set governance (who sees what, alert thresholds) | Consultant (human) | Finalized dashboard, governance document, user access configured |

**What the client sees:** A working KPI dashboard in Domo, benchmarked against their industry, showing the metrics that matter to their specific role. Not a generic "executive dashboard" — but an aerospace supply chain dashboard with C2C cycle, inventory turns, fill rate, DMSMS risk, and AOG resolution time, each with industry benchmarks.

**Phase 2 deliverables:**
- Live KPI dashboard in Domo (5-10 industry-specific KPIs with benchmarks)
- Data pipeline documentation (sources, connectors, refresh schedule)
- Governance framework (user access, alert thresholds, escalation paths)
- KPI definitions document (what each metric means, how it's calculated, what good looks like)

### Phase 3: Deployment — AI CoS Live (Month 2+)

**Goal:** The AI layer goes live. Daily/weekly operational intelligence, delivered via Slack or email.

| Cadence | Activity | Who | Deliverable |
|---------|----------|-----|-------------|
| Daily | **Operational Brief** — KPI status, anomalies detected, items requiring attention | AI | Daily brief (Slack/email) |
| Weekly | **Trend Analysis** — Week-over-week changes, emerging patterns, forecast adjustments | AI | Weekly trend report |
| Monthly | **Strategy Review** — Consultant reviews AI output, provides strategic context, adjusts KPIs | Consultant (human) | Monthly strategy memo |
| Quarterly | **Re-assessment** — Industry research refresh, KPI expansion, dashboard updates | AI + Consultant | Updated engagement prep, dashboard expansion plan |

**What the client sees:** Every morning, a brief in their Slack channel: "C2C cycle improved 3 days this week (now 87 days, was 90). Inventory turns declined to 2.8x (industry median 3.2x). Large order from Boeing delayed — may impact fill rate next week. Recommendation: expedite the SEDNA integration to get real-time inventory data."

The AI doesn't replace the consultant. It does the routine monitoring and pattern detection. The consultant does the interpretation, the strategic advice, and the relationship management. The client gets both — continuous intelligence and human judgment.

### Phase 4: Handover (Month 3-6)

**Goal:** Transfer operational ownership to the client team while maintaining the advisory relationship.

| Activity | Deliverable |
|----------|-------------|
| Client team training | Training materials, recorded sessions, competency checklist |
| Documentation | Runbooks for common operations, KPI definitions, governance docs |
| Support tier agreement | SLA for response times, escalation paths, monthly check-in schedule |
| 30/60/90-day review | Progress assessment, expansion recommendations |

**The handover is NOT the end of the engagement.** The AI CoS continues running. The client team takes over day-to-day dashboard operations, while the consultant remains on retainer for strategic review, KPI expansion, and system evolution.

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **AI accuracy** — Daily briefs contain wrong analysis | Medium | High | Human review of all briefs before delivery. AI flags confidence level. Consultant reviews before client sees. |
| **Data quality** — Client's data is too messy for KPIs | Medium | Medium | Data source mapping in Discovery phase surfaces this early. KPI feasibility matrix scores data quality before committing to build. |

| **Industry coverage** — We only have A&D research cached | High (now) | Low (now) | Each new industry takes 2-4 hours of AI research to cache. The build cost is front-loaded; the reuse is permanent. |

## Appendix A: Engagement Prep Runbook (5-Step Process)

The research process that precedes every Discovery interview:

1. **Industry Assessment** — Broad industry research (KPIs, benchmarks, trends, problems). Cached per industry, reusable across companies. AI-driven via 10 source category searches.
2. **Company Assessment** — Company dossier with business model, leadership, funding, competitive landscape, and data infrastructure map. Per-company.
3. **Industry Pattern Hunter** — Identifies recurring patterns across the industry. What problems are structural? What KPI gaps are common? Reasoning step, not new research.
4. **Company Pattern Hunter** — Identifies company-specific signals. Recent news, hiring patterns, leadership backgrounds, business stage, tech stack signals.
5. **Synthesis** — Combines all four into engagement prep artifacts: pre-interview hypotheses, worry matrix, tailored forcing questions, the wedge, KPI feasibility matrix, interview guide.

All artifacts are saved to the mdrag knowledge base for cross-session retrieval and compounding knowledge.
